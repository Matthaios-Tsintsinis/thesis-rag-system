The Transformer is a neural network architecture introduced by Vaswani et al. in 2017
in the paper "Attention Is All You Need". It dispenses with recurrence and convolution
entirely and relies on a mechanism called self-attention to model dependencies between
input and output tokens regardless of their distance in the sequence.

A Transformer is composed of an encoder stack and a decoder stack. Each layer in either
stack contains a multi-head self-attention sub-layer and a position-wise feed-forward
sub-layer, with residual connections and layer normalisation applied around each. The
decoder additionally includes a masked self-attention sub-layer to prevent attending
to future tokens during training, plus a cross-attention sub-layer that attends to the
encoder output.

Self-attention computes for each token a weighted sum of all token representations in
the sequence, where the weights are derived from compatibility scores between learned
query, key, and value projections. Because attention is parallelisable across positions
in a way that recurrence is not, Transformers train faster than RNNs on long sequences
and have become the dominant architecture for modern language models, including BERT,
GPT, and T5.
